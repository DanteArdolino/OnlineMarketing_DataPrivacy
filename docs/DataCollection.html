Data Collection

When browsing the internet, digital traces are left behind that websites can legally use to keep track of your activities and identify you. The data collected can include; your location, what device you’re using, which advertisements you’ve clicked on, and more.

Privacy settings do not fully protect you when you are inside of a specified browser. Certain information about you is inevitably revealed to the sites you visit. For example, you start sharing your IP address as soon as you go online, which can be used to pinpoint your approximate location.

Your browser also reveals its name, allowing sites to know whether you’re a Firefox, Explorer, or Chrome user. Even seemingly small and tiny details such as the current battery level can be revealed if you’re online from a phone, tablet, or laptop. Other information revealed by your browser can include, what CPU and GPU are you using,which operating system you’re running, as well as the plugins installed and screen resolution

Browser fingerprinting is another method used by sites to identify users even if they don’t enter any personally identifiable information. It involves tracking and detecting the configuration and settings information that browsers make visible. *See page regarding Browser Fingerprinting for more information

Because of the highly improbable likelihood that another person is using the SAME exact specifications as you such as resolution, screen size, plugins and other browser ID, websites can know if you are the same user as before via these specifications
